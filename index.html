🧪 How to do everything in lab 🍪

🍫 Standards

  1. Fill a clean buret with isopropyl alcohol.
  2. Fill the buret with your given solution.

If you are using 10 mL tubes

  1. Pour solution into the cuvette, wipe the non-glazed sides with toilet paper, and place into the Milton.
  2. Fill the buret up to 10 with solution. Find the little beaker labeled 3, fill it with 3 mL of solution, and dilute up to the line with isopropyl alcohol.
  3. INVERT INVERT INVERT! Then pour into the cuvette, wipe the non-glazed sides with toilet paper, and place into the Milton.
  4. Repeat the last two steps for beaker 7, then 4 and 6, then beakers 8 and 9.

If you are using 25 mL tubes

  1. Measure 10 mL of solution into a beaker, dilute with isopropyl up to the line, INVERT INVERT INVERT!, wipe the non-glazed sides with toilet paper, and place into the Milton.
  2. Fill the buret up to 10 with solution. Find the little beaker labeled 3, fill it with 3 mL of solution, and dilute up to the line with isopropyl alcohol.
  3. INVERT INVERT INVERT! Then pour into the cuvette, wipe the non-glazed sides with toilet paper, and place into the Milton.
  4. Repeat the last two steps for beaker 7, then 4 and 6, then beakers 8 and 9.

Then just type each absorbance into the Standards table here and the epsilons calculate themselves.

🍩 Unknowns

  1. Weigh 4 of the 50 mL flasks and write the weights in your notebook.
  2. Do the syringing (small one) at the back and syringe 3 lines.
  3. Put it into the 1st flask.
  4. Take the measurement again and write it in your notebook (it should be around 0.1).
  5. Fill with isopropyl to the line and INVERT INVERT INVERT! Wipe the sides with tp, place into the Milton, and write the value in your book.
  6. Repeat steps 2 to 5 for the remaining flasks.

Figuring out the dilution from your reading

Use the Dilution Factor Finder on the page, or do it yourself:

  1. In the sweet spot (around Standards 5 to 7) → no dilution, use 1/10. If it is in range but near the very top (9/10) or very bottom (3), the finder will still nudge it toward the middle.
  2. Above that range (too concentrated) → dilution = (absorbance of 10 minus absorbance of 3) divided by your reading. Example: if that gives 0.6, your dilution is 6/10. Fill the beaker to the line with isopropyl, INVERT INVERT INVERT! Take a 10 mL flask, and with the red squishy thing and the rod (the one that goes up to 10 mL) bring isopropyl up to the 6 mL line and add that to the 10 mL flask. Top up with isopropyl to the line, INVERT INVERT INVERT!, put into the cuvette, wipe with tp, into the Milton, and read it again.
  3. Below that range (too dilute) → redo it in a 25 mL flask instead of 50. In the Dilution Finder just hit "Switch flasks to 25 mL" and the app handles the math.
Aim for your mass percents to land between about 0.1 and 0.5, and they should all come out close to each other.

🧁 Using this calculator

  1. Pink boxes are what you change, caramel boxes are values from your notebook, vanilla cells are auto-calculated.
  2. Set the top section once (molar mass, mass of solid, dissolution, dilution ratio, tube size).
  3. Standards: type the absorbances, get the epsilons.
  4. Unknowns: type each mass (or empty + filled), absorbance, and dilution. Set the dilution once with "Dilution for all flasks". Each flask auto-grabs the closest standard's epsilon (you can type your own to override).
  5. Your mass percent shows in the big caramel tiles, and "Export CSV" saves everything to a spreadsheet.
